Design of Ball screw Drive system

Personal project

Overview

Project involved the complete design and development of a precision linear actuator from the ground up, tailored for applications requiring accurate and repeatable linear motion. The actuator features a stepper motor-driven ball screw mechanism with a custom-machined carriage, linear guide rails, and a rigid base plate assembly.

Key objectives

Mechanical

  • Designing for high positional accuracy, low backlash, and smooth travel.
  • Performing hand calculations to determine critical parameters such as ball screw lead, shaft diameter, critical speed, buckling load, and motor torque requirements.
  • Ensuring mechanical robustness through careful material selection and component sizing.

    • Highlights

    • Custom Ball screw sizing: Hand-calculated based on Euler buckling and critical speed constraints.
    • Custom End Cap Recirculation For faster recirculation
    • Ball screw drive system: Offers high efficiency and precision over leadscrew alternatives.
    • Integrated linear guideway: Minimizes deflection and improves repeatability.
    • CAD and assembly: Fully modeled in SolidWorks with realistic motion constraints and tolerances considered.
