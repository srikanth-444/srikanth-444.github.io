Design of Ball screw Drive system

Personal project

Overview

Proposed and led the development of an autonomous robot to identify the ballon and pop it, I defined its core architecture — including sensing, actuation, and control — and guided the integration of hardware and software. Directed The team to build a system capable of real-time navigation and precise balloon-popping.

System Integration

Mechanical

  • Helped in modularizing the chassis to house all components, Suggested using Carter pins to ensure ease of accessibility
  • Suggested to Pack all electronics inside the package to avoid accidental damages to wire
  • Incorporated mecanum wheels for omnidirectional movement effectively making Halonomic motion, enabling smooth navigation.

    • Electronics & Sensors

    • Integrated Micro processor and microcontrollers (Raspberry Pi & Arduino Mega) to handle high-level logic and motor control.
    • Implemented sensor fusion with
      • IMU for orientation and acceleration data.
      • Ultrasonic sensors for obstacle detection and avoidance.
      • pixy Camera for ballon detection and tracking.

    Software & Control

    • Developed Python-based control algorithms for real-time decision-making.
    • Implemented custom serial communication between Raspberry Pi and Arduino for efficient data transfer.
    • Optimized communication between Raspberry Pi and Arduino by packaging data from 4 infrared, 2 ultra sound sensors and 1 pixy into a 3-byte payload on the Arduino (Mega), minimizing latency and ensuring efficient, low-overhead transmission.

Future Challenges & solutions

  • Advanced Localization: While The robot is equiped with optical flow sensor and IMU localization was not implemented due to time constraints
  • Power Optimization: Due to continous operation of motor for disk shooter we lost too much of energy making the disk spin only when it used could potention reduce the power consumption.
  • Enhanced Path Planning: Implementing combination of Model Predictive Control (MPC) & RRT would help in smoother navigation.

Results & Learnings

  • Successfully developed a fully autonomous robot with integrated mechanical, electronic, and software systems.
  • Demonstrated effective real-time control
  • Gained hands-on experience in robotics, embedded systems, and system integration.
